Charge by QR · smartphone

For a smartphone customer with data: post an amount → get a QR. They scan it and are charged exactly that - their phone never types the amount. (Feature-phone customers with no internet use the USSD card below instead.)

Feature phone (USSD) · no internet

For a customer with a basic phone and no internet. They dial your till on their own phone - no app, no scanning. Show this code (or tape up the printed sticker); they type the amount and confirm with their mobile-money PIN on the operator's own prompt.

Take a payment

A customer pays this merchant through pawaPay sandbox. The payment lands pending - real money movement is never instant - then confirms automatically via pawaPay's signed callback, usually within seconds. The customer number picks the network and the outcome: numbers ending 789 succeed; other endings trigger specific failures (see the testing guide).

On-net payments - confirm receipt

Same-network payments arrive directly on your mobile money - DRC Pay never touches them. When the operator's SMS says the money landed, tap Confirm received to mark the sale paid. These are merchant-attested, not rail-verified.

    Pending payments - safety net

    Payments confirm automatically via pawaPay's signed callback, so you rarely need this. It's a manual fallback: if a payment is stuck below, it asks pawaPay directly for that payment's status and finishes it - so nothing stays stuck.
